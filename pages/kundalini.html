The Kundalini and the Chakras
The Kundalini

The chakras are believed to be seven major energy centers in the body, originating from ancient Indian traditions like Hinduism and Buddhism. The Sanskrit word chakra translates to "wheel" or "disk," referring to the spinning vortexes of energy that run along the spine. Each chakra is associated with specific nerve bundles and organs, and is believed to influence a person's physical, mental, and emotional well-being.

1 - Root Chakra

The root chakra, or Muladhara, is the first of the seven primary chakras and is considered the foundation of the entire chakra system. Located at the base of the spine, its function is to ground your energy and establish a sense of security, stability, and connection to the Earth.
